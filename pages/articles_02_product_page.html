The problems

// Our navigation is too loud

Layout, colours and typography have a huge influence on what the eye is drawn to, and consequently how information is consumed. Let's not mute some key components like best-buys tables or direct access to products within content with a navbar that doesn't directly converts.

// Underused CTA to products

That beign said (see above), our goal is to get users to comparators. Let's make sure our navbar displays CTA's for most valuable products that stands out from the rest of our main menu items. This button should help our visitor achieve our goal (direct access to products).

// We’re not using active states in your main menu

A simple way to make our website more usable is to remind your visitors of where they are at at all times. We do this with breadcrumbs, but it’s also important to make sure your main menu uses active states. Our main menu should highlight the pages you are on at the moment.

Page views rate is slightly higher on mobile devices, why would we make mobile-user experience so painful ?

Seems that many visitors use this tool, let's give it the space it deserves!

The solution (resize for responsive view)

// Maximise CTA to products

Limit fancy CTA to 1 or 2 options by vertical. Large drop-downs can slow the customer journey by providing too much choice and complexity. The ‘Paradox of choice’.

// test on hover and collapsible mega menu

Collapsible Mega Menu A collapsible mega menu is useful if you have a lot of items in your menu. The advantage of this approach is that it won’t cover up any page content. Instead, it’ll push the page down when the menu opens so that all content remains visible. Not only that, but users can move their mouse anywhere and the menu won’t close on them. This means no more hover tunnels or hard-to-click peripheral items.
